# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting college students and young adults in India. In this type of fraud, scammers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests. A common scenario involves a student receiving a phone call from someone pretending to be a bank official or online seller.

The scammer claims that the student must verify their account or receive a refund. The victim is then asked to click a link or approve a UPI collect request. Once approved, money is instantly transferred from the victim’s bank account to the scammer’s account.

Students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm. Many victims are unaware that approving a collect request sends money instead of receiving it.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes difficulty recovering the money. In many cases, victims fail to report the fraud quickly, making recovery difficult.

This case highlights the importance of awareness, verifying payment requests, and avoiding unknown links or calls from suspicious sources.
